## PURPOSE

Personas translate the audience for this engagement from a list of job titles into specific, recognisable people with goals, constraints, and frustrations. Journey maps then trace those people through the flows they depend on day-to-day, so every requirement and every screen design can be tested against a real scenario instead of an abstract role.

Five personas and four journey maps are included. Personas are composites drawn from discovery conversations, stakeholder interviews, UAT observations, and publicly available FIIT Co. member testimonials. Names for non-sponsor personas are illustrative.

## PERSONAS

### 2.1 Arden — The Owner-Operator

| **ARDEN, THE OWNER-OPERATOR**<br>*Runs the studio day-to-day; needs a master record she can trust.* |  |
|---|---|
| **ROLE** | Gym Manager and Project Sponsor. Primary operational owner for everything inside the four walls of FIIT Co. |
| **CONTEXT** | Manages a twelve-trainer roster, oversees member communications, coordinates the class schedule, and handles the studio's day-to-day operations in-between member-facing work. |
| **GOALS** | Reduce time spent reconciling schedules and rosters; have a single trusted source of truth for operational records; prepare the studio for a second location. |
| **FRUSTRATIONS** | Fragmented spreadsheets, duplicated data entry across MindBody and internal tools, intermittent Microsoft Teams connectivity on Mac, and short windows for deep working sessions. |
| **HOW WE SUPPORT THEM** | Give Arden the Class Management Tool as her operational home, structured written updates between working sessions, and clear decision confirmations inside 24 hours. |
| **PRIMARY TOUCHPOINTS** | Class Management Tool, written status reports, Google Meet working sessions, email. |

### 2.2 Jason — The Head Coach & Founder

| **JASON, THE HEAD COACH & FOUNDER**<br>*Owns the brand voice and the training philosophy.* |  |
|---|---|
| **ROLE** | Founder of FIIT Co. and Executive Owner. Former Canadian Super Middleweight Kickboxing Champion with 37+ years of combat training. |
| **CONTEXT** | Teaches classes on the floor, represents the FIIT brand, and sets the cultural tone of the studio. Decision authority on any member-facing change. |
| **GOALS** | Keep training quality consistent as the team grows; protect the 'don't stop when you're tired' standard; move the studio into the new space with no drop in delivery quality. |
| **FRUSTRATIONS** | Anything that makes the brand feel generic, corporate, or disconnected from the actual training experience. |
| **HOW WE SUPPORT THEM** | Give Jason a brand-aligned website and training standards baked into lesson plan templates. Keep him out of day-to-day operational minutiae. |
| **PRIMARY TOUCHPOINTS** | Customer-facing website, lesson plan templates, occasional brand reviews. |

### 2.3 Maya — The Instructor

| **MAYA, THE INSTRUCTOR**<br>*Teaches four to eight classes a week and builds her own plans.* |  |
|---|---|
| **ROLE** | Instructor on the FIIT Co. roster. Teaches a mix of boxing, kickboxing, HIIT, and conditioning classes. |
| **CONTEXT** | Arrives fifteen minutes before class, checks her lesson plan on her phone from the studio floor, pulls equipment, greets members, and runs the session. |
| **GOALS** | Find her own lesson plans fast, know which equipment is available, and capture class delivery notes without leaving the floor. |
| **FRUSTRATIONS** | Any tool that requires desktop access during class; any UI that assumes a quiet room and a keyboard; losing class notes because she had to switch devices. |
| **HOW WE SUPPORT THEM** | A mobile-first Class Management Tool, personal lesson plan ownership, and a delivery log that can be updated with one hand between rounds. |
| **PRIMARY TOUCHPOINTS** | Class Management Tool (mobile), exercise library, delivery log. |

### 2.4 Heather — The Returning Member

| **HEATHER, THE RETURNING MEMBER**<br>*Three-days-a-week member; considers FIIT her second home.* |  |
|---|---|
| **ROLE** | Long-term member of the studio. Books classes in advance, attends regularly, and brings friends. |
| **CONTEXT** | Works full-time; fits training into her week around work and family obligations. Expects booking, schedule, and trainer information to be accurate and up to date. |
| **GOALS** | Book her classes quickly, know who's teaching, and bring friends in on a referral or guest pass without friction. |
| **FRUSTRATIONS** | Schedule changes that aren't reflected online; broken navigation on the current website; having to ask the front desk for information that should be visible on the site. |
| **HOW WE SUPPORT THEM** | A trustworthy customer-facing website with accurate schedule, trainer, and programme information; a smooth referral flow; a guest pass mechanism that front-desk staff can see in real time. |
| **PRIMARY TOUCHPOINTS** | Customer website, MindBody booking flow, referral flow, guest pass flow, in-studio front desk. |

### 2.5 Sam — The Prospective Member

| **SAM, THE PROSPECTIVE MEMBER**<br>*First-timer considering FIIT after a friend's recommendation.* |  |
|---|---|
| **ROLE** | Prospective member. No combat sports or fitness history. |
| **CONTEXT** | Heard about FIIT from a friend; lands on the website looking for reassurance before booking a trial class. Sensitive to gym culture signals and intimidated by 'real fighter' branding. |
| **GOALS** | Understand if FIIT welcomes beginners, see what a first class is like, and book a low-commitment trial. |
| **FRUSTRATIONS** | Jargon-heavy descriptions, pressure-sales vibes, broken links, and any page that feels like it's built for experienced fighters only. |
| **HOW WE SUPPORT THEM** | A homepage with inclusive language, clear first-class pricing, member testimonials from self-identified beginners, and a visible MindBody deep link for a free intro class. |
| **PRIMARY TOUCHPOINTS** | Customer website homepage, programmes page, pricing page, testimonials, MindBody intro-offer flow. |

## JOURNEY MAPS

Each journey walks the persona through the sequence of moments they experience when they interact with FIIT Co.'s systems. What they do, what they think, where they get stuck, and what this engagement delivers to meet them in that moment.

### 3.1 Sam's Journey — Prospect to First Class

| **Stage** | **What They Do** | **What They Think / Feel** | **Pain Point** | **Our Response** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Aware | Hears about FIIT from a friend or sees the studio from Gerrard Street. | Curious but cautious. Worried the studio is for fighters only. | Doesn't know what to Google; might find the old Squarespace site. | New website homepage with inclusive language and clear brand. |
| Consider | Lands on the website, scrolls the homepage, checks programmes and pricing. | Looking for reassurance about fit and price. | Broken navigation and outdated content on the current site. | Rebuilt website with programmes, schedule, pricing, trainers, and testimonials all one scroll away. |
| Book | Clicks Book a Free Class and lands in the MindBody intro offer flow. | Wants no friction between decision and booking. | Any dead end between site and MindBody. | Deep link straight to the intro offer URL from every major CTA. |
| Attend | Arrives at the studio, checks in, takes their first class. | Nervous; needs a warm welcome and a clear first-timer briefing. | Any inconsistency between the website's promise and the studio experience. | Studio experience is the brand's strength; the website simply needs to match it. |
| Convert | Decides whether to buy a class pack or membership. | Feels welcome; ready to commit if onboarding is easy. | Unclear pricing or confusing pass options. | Website presents the full pricing catalogue and the MindBody purchase flow is one click away. |

### 3.2 Heather's Journey — Booking the Week and Bringing a Friend

| **Stage** | **What They Do** | **What They Think / Feel** | **Pain Point** | **Our Response** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Plan | Opens the website or MindBody to plan the week. | Wants to know who's teaching and what's on. | Out-of-date schedule on the current site. | New website schedule page pulls from an accurate source. |
| Book | Books classes in MindBody. | Trusts the booking system; wants it fast. | Any duplicate or conflicting information between site and MindBody. | Deep link to MindBody from the schedule page; no parallel booking surface. |
| Refer | Wants to bring a friend. | Wants a low-friction referral that rewards both people. | No referral flow on the current site. | Referral system design (BA-12) delivers the flow and reward logic. |
| Attend | Shows up for class, possibly with a friend on a guest pass. | Expects front-desk staff to see the guest pass. | Guest passes get lost in paper or ad-hoc tracking. | Guest pass system design (BA-13) gives front desk a monthly view. |
| Advocate | Leaves reviews; posts to social; recommends the gym. | Feels part of the community. | No visible testimonial platform to contribute to. | New website includes written and video testimonials from real members. |

### 3.3 Maya's Journey — Teaching a Class End-to-End

| **Stage** | **What They Do** | **What They Think / Feel** | **Pain Point** | **Our Response** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-Class | Checks their schedule on mobile before the session. | Wants to know the class size, plan, and equipment needs. | Having to check four different tools. | Class Management Tool schedule page, mobile-optimised. |
| Set Up | Pulls equipment from the inventory. | Needs the equipment to actually be there. | No visibility into what's broken or missing. | Equipment inventory page with maintenance status. |
| Deliver | Teaches the class, follows the lesson plan, adapts for attendees. | Wants the lesson plan accessible without leaving the floor. | Plans trapped in PDFs or other trainers' heads. | Lesson plans page with per-instructor ownership. |
| Log | Records attendance, delivery notes, substitutions. | Wants to finish the log in under two minutes. | Paper attendance sheets that get lost. | Delivery log page with one-hand mobile entry. |
| Review | Reviews their week to plan the next one. | Wants to see their own classes, not everyone's. | Tools that show admin-level noise by default. | Instructor role filters the UI to the trainer's own data. |

### 3.4 Arden's Journey — Running the Weekly Operations Review

| **Stage** | **What They Do** | **What They Think / Feel** | **Pain Point** | **Our Response** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pulse | Opens the tool first thing Monday morning. | Wants a single dashboard view. | No consolidated view today. | Dashboard page with classes delivered, upcoming week, and alerts. |
| Schedule | Confirms the week's schedule is correct. | Needs to spot missing instructor assignments. | Scheduling mistakes discovered mid-week. | Schedule page highlights unassigned classes. |
| Instructor Admin | Adds a new trainer or pauses a departing one. | Wants this to take under five minutes. | Fragmented onboarding paperwork. | Instructor management page with add/edit/suspend. |
| Exercise Library | Reviews new exercises added by instructors. | Wants category discipline and tier control. | Previous library was uncategorised. | Exercise library with category, subcategory, tier governance. |
| Review & Report | Reviews the week's delivery log and prepares a summary. | Wants operational evidence, not anecdotes. | No single place to draw evidence from. | Delivery log page and review page feed the weekly summary. |

## HOW THESE PERSONAS SHAPE THE ENGAGEMENT

- The Class Management Tool prioritises mobile usability because Maya's journey happens on the studio floor, not at a desk.
- The Tool's role-based access control model comes directly from the contrast between Arden's needs and Maya's needs, different people on the same screens.
- The customer website's homepage is written for Sam first and Heather second; members can navigate quickly, but prospects need inclusive, beginner-friendly framing above the fold.
- The referral and guest pass flows exist because Heather's journey explicitly includes bringing friends, and the current site gives her nothing to hand them.
- The Class Management Tool's delivery log page is designed for one-handed mobile entry because Maya logs between rounds.
- Jason's brand authority is protected with copy and visual reviews on the customer website; he is not asked to become an operational reviewer on the Class Management Tool.
